My story

I am a manual therapist/bodyworker attending the four-year program at the Canadian School of Osteopathy Manual Practice in my 3rd year of studies. I practice out of Madhu Wellness Collective, a multidisciplinary health clinic and yoga studio. My therapeutic approach is informed through my years of post-secondary study, sport and athletic conditioning, and self-study of a variety of healing modalities.

In 2016, desperate to escape the city and hungry for mountain adventures, I stumbled upon Rossland. The minute I drove through our one and only stoplight to find beautiful fall colors, misty mountains, and welcoming people, I knew I had to find a way to stay. Within the next month, I began working at the Kootenay Boundary Regional Hospital as a registered nurse, and practiced there for four years prior to beginning my journey as a manual therapist.

I have always been fascinated by the complexity of the human body and its ability to heal, and this led me to university to become a registered nurse in 2011. In my career as an RN I primarily worked in labor and delivery/maternity units, pediatrics, and public health, but had experience caring for all age ranges and various medical conditions from nursing school and as a result of working within a rural setting.

After experiencing significant burnout, I decided to switch careers in 2019 to a more preventative healing approach. I felt a lack of fulfillment working within the confines of the traditional medical system. Over the years I felt my frustration grow with the systems reactive nature towards only acute health problems and my lack of time as a nurse to address the whole body as a complex, interconnected unit. After considering various other manual therapy and healing careers, I settled on osteopathic manual therapy.

I discovered osteopathy in my own wellness journey after having two car accidents and experiencing several serious injuries from figure skating, mountain biking, and skiing. I found that when I added osteopathy to my team of multidisciplinary practitioners, I experienced the most significant improvement in my long-term pain and symptoms.

This discipline caught my attention because of its understanding of the intricate anatomy of the human body, the bodys interconnectedness, and belief that the body is capable of self healing and regulation; something that was a primary focus of my nursing education, but near impossible to implement in our current system.

As a student of manual osteopathy and a practitioner, I aim to help my clients find health and wellness as my osteopathic manual practitioner helped me. With my offerings, I hope to make this form of manual therapy more accessible and approachable to our community with my affordable student rates. Additionally, by addressing the body as a functional, interconnected unit, my goal is to prevent symptoms and pain from returning.
