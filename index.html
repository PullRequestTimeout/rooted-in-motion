My frequently asked questions


Manual therapy is an all-encompassing term for health disciplines that manually treat concerns related to the musculoskeletal system (bones and muscles) and styles of treatment vary based on a therapists background and training.

My style is influenced by my background in nursing and my current training in osteopathic manual therapy. I treat and assess the whole body, even if you have pain or lack of mobility limited to one area of the body. With this approach, I aim to prevent your pain or symptoms from returning so you dont have to keep coming back to see me. Instead, some people choose to make appointments for occasional maintenance or just if a new issue presents itself.


As of May 2022, I have completed all my courses for the structural body (muscles, fascia, bones, joints). The final two years of my program are in more depth on the organ systems and some complex issues surrounding the structural body.

With these skills, I can be quite effective with many symptoms or concerns that my clients may have. Of course, if I ever feel out of my depth or if a client is wanting a different approach, I am happy to refer to an osteopathic manual practitioner or any other practitioner necessary.


At this time, sessions with me are not billable through benefits plans, however I charge an affordable rate to compensate.

As the school I am attending is one recognized by Osteopathy BC, once I have completed my osteopathic manual therapy studies (est. Fall 2024) I will be billable through most plans.

Each session involves discussion of your health history, a thorough head to toe assessment, and treatment of any restricted areas of the body. Ill assess and treat areas of the body where youre experiencing symptoms, as well as any areas that might be compensating.

Technique styles performed depend on the clients concerns, symptoms, and medical history but may include gentle joint mobilization, cranial tension balancing, myofascial release, tender point release, targeted muscle activation, and some visceral (internal organs) techniques.

Upon leaving the session most people feel more relaxed and mobile. It is normal to have some short-term aggravation of symptoms, soreness, or awareness of new discomfort 1-3 days after treatment. If you ever feel like your symptoms are out of the ordinary, I encourage you to contact me.
